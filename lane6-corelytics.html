Deep Dive

What Handoff
Actually Looks Like

Four scenarios covering how Lane6 delivers a qualified lead to Corelytics — from fully self-serve to high-touch coordinated intros.

Standing rule across all scenarios: Lane6 owns the lead and the relationship through handoff. Corelytics enters at the demo stage only. Commission tracks to Lane6 on close, regardless of how many nurture cycles the lead required.
A
Scenario A
Warm Intro via Email
Trigger
Lead completes the Exit-Ready Quiz or Financial Plan Scorecard on a Lane6-built tool and meets qualification criteria.
Lane6 Action
Sends a personalized intro email to Alec or Steve at Corelytics with full lead context embedded — pain point, AUM range, ERP environment, and intent score.
Handoff Method
Direct email introduction with structured context brief.
Corelytics Entry
Picks up from the intro email. Lane6 retains ownership of the contact record in CRM until close is confirmed.
💡
Commission tracking: Lead record flagged with Lane6 source at creation. Commission triggers automatically on close confirmation.
B
Scenario B
Self-Serve Demo Registration
Trigger
Lead clicks through a Lane6 nurture touchpoint and self-registers at dashboard.corelytics.com/?mode=register&affiliate=LANE6.
Lane6 Action
Flags the lead in CRM with source attribution, intent score, and full touchpoint history prior to registration.
Handoff Method
Self-directed — lead arrives at Corelytics' registration flow via tagged Lane6 link.
Corelytics Entry
Works the inbound registration. All context is available via CRM flag.
🔗
Commission tracking: Automatic via affiliate tag LANE6 embedded in registration URL. No manual reconciliation needed.
C
Scenario C
High-Intent Coordinated Intro Call
Trigger
Lead shows strong multi-touchpoint signal across Lane6 sequences but has not self-booked. Intent score warrants direct coordination.
Lane6 Action
Briefs Corelytics on the lead, coordinates timing, and facilitates a warm intro call. Lane6 may join the first 2–3 minutes to hand context directly, then exits.
Handoff Method
Live facilitated intro with verbal context transfer. Lead ownership formally transfers at the moment Lane6 exits the call.
Corelytics Entry
Receives the lead live with Lane6's brief. Continues into demo and close independently.
🎙️
Commission tracking: Lead flagged in CRM prior to call. Source attribution confirmed at handoff. Commission triggers on close.
D
Scenario D
Stalled Lead Re-Engagement
Trigger
Lead went cold post-handoff or never converted from the nurture sequence. Inactivity window exceeded threshold.
Lane6 Action
Pulls the lead back into an automated re-engagement sequence, re-qualifies against current criteria, and monitors for refreshed intent signal before re-presenting.
Handoff Method
Re-handoff occurs only after intent is confirmed again — same handoff protocols apply (email intro, self-serve, or coordinated call).
Corelytics Entry
Re-presented to Corelytics as a re-qualified lead with updated context and refreshed intent score.
♻️
Commission tracking: Preserved Regardless of re-engagement cycles, commission tracks to Lane6 on final confirmed close via original lead attribution.
